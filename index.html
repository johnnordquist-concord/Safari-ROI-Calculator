Methodology & Formula Reference

📐
  • ITP 2.1–2.3 (2018–2019): Third-party cookies blocked entirely. JS-set first-party cookies capped at 7 days.
  • ITP 2.3 (2019): 1-day cap introduced for users who arrive via a bounce from a "known tracker" domain — this directly penalises paid ad traffic. If someone clicks a paid ad and does not convert within 24 hours, the cookie is gone.
  • Earlier Safari privacy groundwork: Link Tracking Protection (LTP) strips common click IDs from URLs in private browsing, email, and messaging surfaces.
  • Safari 26 (Sept 2025): Advanced Fingerprinting Protection (AFP) moves from Private Browsing to default ON for all browsing. Blocks canvas fingerprinting, audio fingerprinting, screen entropy APIs, and limits cross-origin referrer access.
  • February 2026: Safari expands ITP's most aggressive protections from Private Browsing → standard Safari browsing. LTP now strips tracking click IDs (gclid, fbclid, msclkid) in regular sessions. UTM parameters (utm_source, utm_medium, utm_campaign) are not stripped.

Unit note: The formulas below use whichever mode is selected above — Users or Sessions.

Step 1 — Observed returning:

safari_ret_obs = max(0, safari_total − safari_new_obs)

Step 2 — Expected returning (3 methods, in priority order):

Method A (Proxy): other_ret_share = other_ret / other_total safari_ret_expected = safari_total × other_ret_share Method B (Manual override): safari_ret_expected = safari_total × (manual_ret_share / 100) Method C (Uplift fallback): fallback_base = max(safari_ret_obs, safari_total × 5%) safari_ret_expected = min(safari_total, fallback_base × 1.25) Guardrail: if Method A or B lands at / below observed Safari returning, use the same conservative floor to avoid zeroing modeled loss.

Step 3 — Lost returning audience:

lost_ret = max(0, safari_ret_expected − safari_ret_obs)

Step 4 — AFP adjustment (Safari 26+ users):

afp_factor = 1 + (safari26_share / 100) × (afp_severity / 100) lost_ret_adj = lost_ret × afp_factor

Step 5 — Recoverable audience:

rec_ret = lost_ret_adj × recoverable_rate

Step 6 — Effective returning CR (priority ladder):

1st: cr_ret if provided 2nd: cr_overall × 1.20 3rd: DEFAULT 1.5%

Step 7 — Revenue outputs:

measurement_value = rec_ret × effective_cr × aov incremental_value = rec_ret × retargetable_share × lift_abs × aov total_opp = measurement_value + incremental_value

  • Server-side Tag Management: Move tag execution to your own first-party domain. This makes measurement less dependent on fragile client-side identifiers and more resilient through first-party server events. Concord can help with this across a wide range of server-side tagging architectures and implementation patterns.
  • Server-set First-Party Cookies (HTTP Set-Cookie): Persist beyond ITP's 7-day JS cap and the 1-day ad-click limit. Requires your own domain/server infrastructure.
  • Server-side Click ID Rescue: Capture gclid/fbclid server-side before Safari's Feb 2026 LTP strips them. Concord can help implement this with the approach and tooling that best fits your environment, including packaged accelerators or custom patterns.
  • Login / Identity Graph: Authenticated users are always identifiable regardless of browser. Even email capture (soft login) can stitch sessions across ITP resets.
  • Platform-side Conversion APIs: Send consented first-party signals directly to advertising platforms to recover attribution credit lost in Safari journeys.
  • Privacy-Preserving Browser Attribution APIs: Use browser-native, privacy-aware attribution methods where they fit your measurement needs and operating model.
